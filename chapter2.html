Chapter 2 - Jonah

    It’s one AM by the time we reach Norton.
    If I was exhausted at the start of the trip, six hours of driving has certainly not helped. The internet charitably describes I-70 as “uneventful highway.” Usually, uneventful suits me just fine. But staying awake at the wheel took more effort than I care to admit. On more than one occasion, I found myself wishing for a giant yarnball, or a famous world monument replicated in junked cars, or some other equally ridiculous roadside attraction. Anything to break up the tedium of endless wheatfield.
    To your credit, you did offer to drive. I supposed you meant it as a kindness, but I didn’t bother dignifying it with a response. You don’t have a license, and having experienced your driving before, I can confidently say you 
shouldn’t.1
